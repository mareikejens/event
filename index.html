PROMPT 2

Label cycle days & phases

Send this

Now, using the merged table and real calculations (never estimates):
1. Find the first day of each cycle (a period day that follows days without a period).
2. Number every day within its cycle (day 1, day 2, ...).
3. Label every day with a phase: menstrual (days 1-5), follicular, ovulatory (3 days centered 14 days before next period), luteal.

Show me the first 10 rows with cycle day and phase, and tell me how many complete cycles you found and how long each was.

✓ You should see: 6 full cycles (may say 7 — a 7th is just starting), 27–30 days each. Full expected result →

PROMPT 3

Compute averages

Send this

Now, with real calculations:

Make a table with the average skin temperature, resting heart rate, HRV and sleep hours for each of the four phases.

✓ You should see: HRV drops sharply in the 2 days before every period — about 49 ms vs about 58 ms the rest of the luteal phase. Your wearable sees your period coming. Temperature steps up about 0.35 °C after ovulation and falls again just before the period. Full expected result →

PROMPT 4

Insights in plain language

Send this

In plain English, no technical terms:
1. What does my data say about how my body changes across my cycle?

2. Is there any stretch in these six months where something unusual happened? If yes: when, what do you see, and what everyday explanations fit?

3. What is one thing I could pay attention to in my next cycle?

✓ You should see: a summary in everyday words — spotting the 16–22 March stress week where HRV crashed, sleep collapsed and resting heart rate spiked. Full expected result →
