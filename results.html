AFTER PROMPT 4

The story, in plain English

No two AIs write this the same way — but a good answer covers these three things:

1 · How your body changes across the cycle

"In the first half of each cycle your body runs cool and recovered: temperature low, heart rate low, recovery (HRV) at its best. After ovulation everything shifts — you run about a third of a degree warmer, your heart beats a few beats faster at rest, and recovery dips. The last two days before each period are the low point: recovery drops clearly and sleep gets shorter — and then day one of the period resets everything."

2 · The unusual stretch

"One week stands out: 16–22 March. Recovery collapsed far below anything hormonal, sleep fell to around six hours, and resting heart rate jumped — right in the middle of a cycle, so the cycle can't explain it. Your own app entries that week say 'stressed'. A rough week at work, an illness brewing, or travel would all fit."

3 · One thing to watch next cycle

"Watch the two days before your period: when recovery dips and sleep shortens, that's your body's heads-up. Plan those days a little lighter — and see if the pattern holds for you."

The AI plays detective — you judge whether the story fits. It should find the March week on its own and use the mood entries as supporting evidence. Everyday explanations are hypotheses, not diagnoses.
